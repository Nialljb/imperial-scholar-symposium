About this symposium

The important thing is to never stop questioning – Albert Einstein

In the past decades and centuries, scientists were able to throw off the yoke of established knowledge and break new ground, in order to make a mark on their particular field. As our knowledge has advanced as a species and the basics of many of these fields have been refined over and over, eminent scientists have expressed the view that science has somehow “finished”, or has succeeded in laying the foundations for understanding the world we find ourselves in.

However, history has proven these assertions spectacularly wrong, without fail!

So, which contemporary scientific facts are currently under scrutiny, and where might the next revolutions lie? What are the burgeoning areas of engineering, science and medicine? How do we push the boundary of contemporary research into new frontiers?

The Imperial College Scholars’ PhD Symposium 2017 presents international speakers from a diverse range of fields, and a wealth of graduate student research, to explore these questions. Do you want a glimpse of the possibilities that get scientists excited? Do you want to listen to the predictions for the next big changes and innovations, in the 21st century and beyond?

If so, this is the event to attend!

Keynote Speakers

Professor Peter Childs

Professor Peter Childs is Head of the Dyson School of Design Engineering at Imperial College London. General interests include: creativity tools and innovation; design; heat transfer, rotating flow and sustainable energy. Former roles include director of the Rolls-Royce University Technology Centre for Aero-Thermal Systems, director of InQbate and professor at Sussex University. He has contributed to over 150 papers, and several books including the Handbook on Mechanical Design Engineering and books on rural urban migration and sports technology. He has been principal or co-investigator on contracts totalling £30 million. His roles at Imperial include joint course director for the Innovation Design Engineering and Global Innovation Design degrees with the Royal College of Art, and Design Lead for the Manufacturing Futures Lab. He is Director and Chief Scientific Officer at QBot Ltd.

Dr. Simon Foster

Simon studied Physics with Space science (so he’s a qualified rocket scientist) at the University of Southampton and stayed on to undertake a PhD in Solar-terrestrial Physics, specialising in solar variability and climate change. Simon has taught physics and astronomy in primary and secondary schools and is currently a solar physicist and outreach officer for the Department of Physics at Imperial College London. Simon competed in the 2009 FameLab competition winning the London heat and coming 3rd in the national final, held at the Cheltenham science festival. Since the competition, Simon has talked at various science and music festivals around the UK, as well as presenting TV and Radio shows for the Sky, BBC and Channel 4 as well as having a video column in the Telegraph.

Professor Robert Winston

Lord Winston is Professor of Science and Society and Emeritus Professor of Fertility Studies at Imperial College London. In the 1970s he developed gynaecological surgical techniques that improved fertility treatments. He later pioneered new treatments to improve in vitro fertilisation (IVF) and developed pre-implantation diagnosis. This allowed embryos to be screened for genetic diseases and has allowed parents carrying faulty genes to have children free of illnesses such as cystic fibrosis.He now runs a research programme at the Institute of Reproductive and Developmental Biology at Imperial College that aims to improve human transplantation. Robert Winston has over 300 scientific publications about human reproduction and the early stages of pregnancy.Robert Winston is also Chairman of the Genesis Research Trust – a charity which raised over £13 million to establish the Institute of Reproductive and Developmental Biology and which now funds high quality research into women’s health and babies.
